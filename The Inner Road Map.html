Energy Centers

The Seven Chakras

Each energy center holds a distinct power, fear, and spiritual invitation — mapped here through Caroline Myss, Ismaili scripture, and the Ismaili hierarchy of souls.

7th · Sahasrara · Universal Soul

Crown

Spiritual Power — Union with the Divine

Energetic Patterns

Disconnection, spiritual cynicism, withdrawal from meaning

Core Fear

Meaninglessness, death, loss of faith in a greater order

Ismaili Hierarchy

Universal Soul → Imam

Faith over reason — not blind belief, but trust in something greater than the ego. Devote yourself to purpose, practice gratitude, and live from a place of service.

In the Dua

When we invoke "Shah Karim al-Husayni... have mercy upon us" — we move beyond seeing into surrender. The living Imam as embodiment of Divine Light is 7th chakra territory. "O Allah to Thee is my prostration and obedience." The veil lifts. The self bows. Union begins.

6th · Ajna · Intellect (Spirit)

Third Eye

Intuitive Power — Insight, Perception, Inner Vision

Energetic Patterns

Illusion, denial, projection, seeing only surface reality

Core Fear

Seeing the truth — in self or others — fear of inner guidance

Ismaili Hierarchy

Intellect (Spirit) → Bab (Gate)

Trust your intuition and perception. Meditation, self-inquiry, and symbolic sight — seeing beyond the literal — strengthen this center. Learn to see patterns, not just events.

In the Dua — Surat-ul-Ikhlas

"Say: He is Allah, the One! Allah, the eternally Besought of all!" — Pure perception. The Ajna invites us to perceive unity behind multiplicity. Shah Jo Deedar: may your third eye awaken. The ego surrenders so deeper wisdom can come through.

5th · Vishuddha · Rational Soul

Throat

Will Power — Communication, Truth, Divine Will

Energetic Patterns

Lies, secrets, fear of judgment, swallowing truth

Core Fear

Not being heard, silenced truth, disempowerment

Ismaili Hierarchy

Rational Soul → Hujja (Proof)

Align your personal will with divine will. Be truthful in speech, express needs with clarity, surrender control over outcomes. Truth heals; lies deplete energy.

In the Dua — Right Alignment

"O ye who believe, do not betray Allah and the Apostle... while you know." Conscious moral agency — a rational soul function. "O 'Aly, O Muhammad" — this repetition is not just supplication but invocation, a 5th chakra function.

4th · Anahata · Imaginal Soul

Heart

Emotional Power — Love, Compassion, Forgiveness

Energetic Patterns

Grief, heartbreak, conditional love, jealousy

Core Fear

Loneliness, rejection, emotional pain

Ismaili Hierarchy

Imaginal Soul (Khayal/Gabriel) → Da'i (Summoner)

The heart is the bridge between physical and spiritual realms. Practice forgiveness as a way of releasing toxic energy and returning to balance. Love is a choice to stay open and clear.

Imaginal Soul — The Bridge

Trust, loyalty, intimacy. The Imaginal Soul sits between the animal and rational — one face toward sense perception, one toward the intellect. Like the horse (khayl) and imagination (khayal) sharing an Arabic root: the heart holds both worlds.

3rd · Manipura · Animal Soul

Solar Plexus

Personal Power — Self-Esteem, Confidence, Identity

Energetic Patterns

Shame, victimhood, low self-worth, giving power away

Core Fear

Rejection, criticism, failure

Ismaili Hierarchy

Animal Soul (Fath/Michael) → Mu'allim (Teacher)

Self-esteem is the strongest personal power. Stop giving your power away to others' opinions and instead act from your own inner authority. Clean up where you hold resentment — these leak your power.

In the Dua — Courage

"O Apostle, deliver what has been revealed to thee from thy Lord... Allah will protect thee from the people." Animal Soul = courage in place of fear of rejection, criticism, or failure. The master rides the nafs rather than being ruled by it.

2nd · Svadhisthana · Vegetative Soul

Sacral

Relationship Power — Sexuality, Finances, Creativity

Energetic Patterns

Guilt, dependency, control, power vs. powerlessness duality

Core Fear

Loss of control, fear of betrayal or financial ruin

Ismaili Hierarchy

Vegetative Soul → Mukasir (Breaker)

Emotional honesty and integrity, especially in intimate relationships. Observe how power dynamics show up, and choose to act with dignity rather than manipulation.

Plant Soul — Establishing Peace

"O Allah, O our Lord, Thou art the peace, and from Thee is the peace... give us life of peace." Right relationship and structure; recognition of authority. Establishing peace for emotions to flow. "It is safe to feel and express."

1st · Muladhara · Mineral Soul

Root

Tribal Power — Safety, Belonging, Family

Energetic Patterns

Loyalty, survival, traditions, generational beliefs

Core Fear

Abandonment, loss of physical safety or support

Ismaili Hierarchy

Mineral Soul → Mustajib (Respondent)

Examine inherited beliefs from family and culture: "Do these support my growth or limit me?" Let go of what no longer serves your personal truth. Self-responsibility is the foundation.

Mineral Soul — Foundation

The most primordial level of the soul — cohesion, structure, the capacity to hold form. In Gurdjieff's language: E10 Cohesive Energy — binding energies at the very basis of matter. The lattice that everything else rests upon.

The Horse
& The Rider

Khayl and khayal share one Arabic root. The horse and the imagination are one symbol — both wild until tamed, both capable of carrying the soul toward the divine when ridden with mastery.

خيل
Khayl
The Horse — power, wildness, directionality
خيال
Khayal
Imagination — thought, contemplation, inner vision
According to the science of ta'wil (spiritual exegesis), Arabic words sharing a root indicate symbolic relationships between their referents. The horse in the animal realm symbolizes the faculty of imagination in the spiritual realm.

Gurdjieff's Three Foods — The Inner Alchemy of Transformation

I

Ordinary Food

The Body's Kitchen

Coarse food maintains the bodily organism — it circulates and does no more than sustain the vessel. It is the source of basic energy by which transformation comes, but it is not the food itself. The kitchen's utensils kept in order.

Corresponds to the Animal Soul — the body's instinctual maintenance, the wild horse grazing

II

Air · Breath

"Our second being-food"

It is not bread but breath that feeds the soul. Anima, ruach, atman, prana, nefesh — across all traditions, breath and soul share one word. Something has to happen to air so that it becomes fixed. The transformation of breath has been lost to the West.

Corresponds to the Imaginal Soul — refined through pranayama and dhikr, breath becomes the vehicle of the khayal, refining imagination upward

III

Impressions

Spiritual food

Our spiritual food is distilled from our experience of life. What matters is where our Will is in our mode of perceiving — how far are we detached and free? How far are we able to be our own Cooks? This is the highest food, and the hardest to assimilate.

Corresponds to the Rational Soul and beyond — the Hujja receiving knowledge through khayal from the Imam: the highest impressions, beyond the senses

∞

The three foods — body, breath, impressions — mirror the three components of all existence: function (the cyclical body), being (breath, always integrating or disintegrating, never neutral), and will (impressions received by the soul, the one will fragmenting into many or many wills integrating toward one). Man is a cosmic apparatus for the transformation of these three energies. The horse of imagination, when ridden well, is the very instrument by which the highest impressions — transmitted from Imam to Hujja through khayal — are received and carried downward into the world.

Gurdjieff · Bennett · Nasir-i Khusraw · Synthesized

Prayer & Physiology

Dua as Nervous
System Intervention

Reciting Dua isn't just a spiritual act — it's a nervous system intervention, done at the exact moments the body is most impressionable and sensitive to regulation.

Daily Nervous System Rhythms & Dua as Transition Markers

Mobilization / Alertness (Sympathetic)
Rest / Integration (Parasympathetic)

~5:00 AM · Dawn

Fajr Dua

الفجر

Cortisol ↑ Rising

As cortisol spikes to wake you, this transitional state between delta/theta and alpha/beta is when you are most suggestible. Intentions set here become the day's tone. Grounding at Fajr replaces stress-locking with aligned alertness.

Brainwave state: theta → alpha transition

~6:30 PM · Sunset

Maghrib Dua

المغرب

Cortisol ↓ · Melatonin ↑

Light decreases; the body downregulates from sympathetic activation. Melatonin rises, making you more sensitive to emotional content and less buffered by alertness. This is often when sadness, fatigue, or vulnerability surfaces — and most needs a container.

The ache of sunset calls for gentle anchoring

~8:00 PM · Night

Isha Dua

العشاء

Parasympathetic ↑ Dominant

The body wants to surrender into full parasympathetic dominance, which peaks during deep sleep at 2–4 AM. Isha provides a regulated pathway into rest — a ritual signal: "You are safe, you are connected, and it's safe to let go."

Brainwave state: returning toward theta/delta

The nervous system wants rituals like this. Predictable cues that say: You are safe, you are connected, and it's safe to let go.

A Living Testimony · March 7, 2026

An Experience of
Ego Dissolution

The maps in this prototype are not abstract. They describe something real — something that happens, that has happened, that is happening now. This is one account of it.

March 7, 2026 · Written the morning after

I was born into Islamic geometry. Every jamatkhana I ever attended held it. I didn't study it or seek it. It was simply the visual language of what safe, rhythmic, and sacred felt like.

What follows is the account of a four-hour dissolution that began while lying on a couch, looking up at a painting on the wall. The geometry was the portal. The music was the vehicle. Eight weeks of twice-daily meditation practice — spinal breathing pranayama followed by mantra — was the preparation. And Rehman, present and steady in the corner of the room, was the island keeper.

This is what the frameworks in this site are pointing toward. Not concepts to understand. A reality to enter.

Born
Jamatkhana

The patterns were present at every prayer, every gathering, every sacred moment of community. The visual language of what safe, rhythmic, and sacred felt like.

2013
Alhambra · Granada & Lisbon Jamatkhana

Something shifted into conscious awareness at the Alhambra. Then the luminous tiled wall of the Lisbon jamatkhana — thousands of tiny particles of blue and gold shimmering together into something alive.

2016
Tomb of Hafez · Shiraz, Iran

"My eyes are mesmerized by the intricate patterns. It's a mirror for my own soul and the Soul of the Universe, both of which seem to be lost in each other. And at last, I relinquish the desire to distinguish the real from its reflection."

2022
The Painting · A gift from Ikrah

A gift of Islamic geometry hung on the wall. When she gave it, the price — $400 — felt unbelievable as an act of generosity. "At that time, I wasn't sure I would have spent that much on myself." That painting became the portal.

2026
Home · The Experience

Lying on the couch. Rehman as DJ. Eight weeks of practice. The song began. The music had found my body before my mind had finished deciding.

The Experience — In Stages

🎵

The Invitation

Mein · Tu Jhoom · Mehram — songs soaked in Urdu poetry and the mystical dimension. When the first song began: "The pull. The invitation. Something in me recognized — I am being taken." A quiet internal question: can I go? Is all else okay? He was there. All was well.

🌬

The Breath Surrenders

The music had found the body before the mind had finished deciding. "My breath was no longer mine. The song was breathing me." Beneath everything, the Ism-e-Azam was running — not recited but self-generating, absorbed through eight weeks of twice-daily meditation. The reed Rumi wrote about. Not playing the music. Being played by it.

✦

The Geometry Shifts

Eyes fixed on the painting. Nothing else felt right to look at. It moved from 2D to 3D to "something I can only describe as 5D — because I stopped looking at it and became it." The gold specks came apart. Infinite space between each particle. Dancing, swirling. A speck of gold dust inside a Sheesh Mahal — a house of mirrors — from all angles simultaneously.

∞

Particle to Space

"I shifted from being a particle in the dance to being the space that held it all. Infinite. Unlocatable. The stillness within which everything was moving." Gazing at the luminous pattern, wondering what made it glow so brightly, what it was reflecting — it was me. I was the source of the light. The universe recognizing itself as the light, not just the observer.

🌍

Worlds Arising and Dissolving

Whole worlds emerged and dissolved. Not just watching — making it happen. Occasionally Rehman appeared at the corner of the visual frame — the "island keeper" — and would pull momentarily back: oh yes, a body, a home, a name, a husband. Then he would leave the frame and the worlds would begin again. After four hours of what appeared from the outside as complete stillness: "What else would I be doing?" — responding, without knowing it, as the Universe.

You are the universe looking at its own reflection.

The one insight that came back from the experience · March 6, 2026
